# 𝕊-FLM Sudoku Reproduction

Reproduction of the **Sudoku** results from *Language Modeling with Hyperspherical Flows*
(Deschenaux & Gulcehre, [arXiv:2605.11125](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11125), Table 1),
using this repository's training/eval scripts.

## Setup

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Model | `tiny-sphere-dit` (sphere-DiT), 8 blocks, hidden 512, 8 heads, seq-len 180, 28.6 M params |
| Training | 20,000 steps, global batch 256, AdamW lr 3e-4, EMA 0.9999, bf16 |
| Data | 48,000 train / 2,000 valid puzzles per difficulty (seed 42); generated by `sudoku_generator.py` |
| Difficulties | easy = 40 clues, medium = 35, hard = 30 |
| Eval | `mode=sudoku_eval`, 180 sampling steps, `velocity=exact`, `noise_removal=greedy`, `top_k_velocity=-1`, EMA on; full 2,000 valid puzzles; metric = exact match of all 81 solution cells |
| Hardware | 1× NVIDIA RTX A6000 (per run ~2.5 h, GPU-bound) |
| Logging | Weights & Biases (online), project `wandb.ai/syctw/debug` |

The three variants correspond to the paper's rows and the repo scripts:

| Variant | Script | Noise schedule |
|---|---|---|
| **base** | `scripts/train/sudoku/sfm.sh` | `log-linear` (vanilla, no truncation) |
| **truncated** | `scripts/train/sudoku/sfm_truncated.sh` | `log-linear`, `alpha_max=0.093` |
| **truncated+adaptive** | `scripts/train/sudoku/sfm_truncated_adaptive.sh` | `log-linear-adaptive`, `alpha_max=0.093`, refit every 50 steps |

## Bug found and fixed: adaptive schedule inactive at eval

While auditing, a multi-agent adversarial review found that the **adaptive noise schedule
was silently inactive at checkpoint-load eval**:

- Lightning's `load_from_checkpoint` calls `load_state_dict` on the **top-level** module,
  which copies `AdaptiveSchedule`'s registered buffers in place but **never dispatches to
  `AdaptiveSchedule.load_state_dict`** — the only code that rebuilt the alpha spline.
- With `_alpha_spline is None`, `AdaptiveSchedule.alpha_t` fell back to the base (truncated)
  schedule, byte-for-byte. Every "adaptive" eval was therefore a *truncated* run.

**Fix** (`noise_schedules.py`, +15 lines): lazy rebuild — `_maybe_rebuild_spline()` is called at
the top of `alpha_t`/`alpha_prime_t`, reconstructing the spline from the `alpha_vals` buffer
when `_alpha_spline is None and has_schedule`. No-op during training.

Re-evaluating with the fix changed the adaptive numbers by < 0.6 pt, because the schedule the
training *learned* is distinct in shape but ≈ truncated in accuracy. **So the adaptive-medium
gap is genuine, not an artifact of the bug.**

## Assessment of the two gaps

- **base-medium (39.0 vs 50.6):** high seed variance (11-pt spread across 3 seeds) plus a
  downward bias. Base 𝕊-FLM is the paper's acknowledged-unstable baseline; it matched at hard
  (13.85/14.0) and was close at easy.
- **adaptive-medium (78.2 vs 85.2):** low variance (~78% both seeds), ≈ its own truncated
  (77.9%). The adaptive refit here does not reproduce the paper's distinctive **+7.6** medium
  boost over truncated.

Both gaps are most plausibly explained by this repo's `sudoku_generator.py` differing from the
paper's PRISM data-prep reference (truncated is simply more robust to it). The eval protocol was
adversarially verified faithful (180 steps, exact velocity, greedy, EMA, correct 20k
checkpoints, full 2,000-puzzle set).

**Reproduced cleanly:** the paper's central claim that truncating the noise schedule transforms
𝕊-FLM (base → truncated lifts medium 39 → 78% and hard 14 → 48%), and truncated matches/exceeds
the paper at all three difficulties.
