Sudoku solve-rate with LangFlow-style self-conditioning
(`algo.self_conditioning=true`, `p_self_cond=0.25`); protocol and reference
numbers in EXPERIMENT.md. Phase 1 = 9-cell hard pilot (2026-07-13); phase 2 =
full jul02-slides grid (tables auto-generated by analyze.py below).

## Insights / conclusions

- **Self-conditioning transfers to all three geometries, with gains of the
  same magnitude as LangFlow's (+32 pt):** +27.0 pt for S-FLM+trunc+ada,
  +26.5 pt for E-FLM, +31.5 pt for H-FLM. The hypothesis is confirmed.
- **Ordering with SC: S-FLM+trunc+ada (69.2) > H-FLM (55.6) > LangFlow
  (50.4) > E-FLM (45.7).** SC preserves the geometry ranking and
  S-FLM+trunc+ada+SC is the new best sudoku-hard number in the repo,
  well above the paper-target 42.2.
- E-FLM+SC (45.7) more than doubles its no-SC baseline and lands near
  LangFlow+SC — consistent with SC compensating for weak iterative
  refinement in flat geometry.
- Seed variance stays small (≤3.6 pt std), comparable to the no-SC
  baselines, so the gains are far outside noise.

## Phase 2 insights (full grid, completed 2026-07-13)

- **Self-conditioning improves every cell of the grid**: 18/18 (model ×
  difficulty × LR) comparisons are positive, from +8.1 to +34.6 pt. The
  LangFlow trick is fully geometry-agnostic.
- **New repo-best numbers, all beating LangFlow+SC (87.2 med / 50.4 hard):**
  S-FLM+trunc+ada+SC **92.6 med / 69.7 hard**, H-FLM+SC **91.0 med /
  65.6 hard**. E-FLM+SC (82.7 / 45.7) roughly matches LangFlow+SC on hard
  despite no adaptive schedule.
- **SC shrinks seed variance**, answering the jul02 deck's "can we stabilize
  H-FLM?" — hard hflm@3e-4 goes 46.2 ± 13.1 → 65.0 ± 4.6, medium
  sfm@3e-4 76.7 ± 8.9 → 92.6 ± 1.1.
- **SC flattens LR sensitivity**: with SC, hflm hard spans 64.0–65.6 across
  LRs (vs 31.0–46.2 without); sfm medium spans 91.3–92.6. Tuning matters much
  less once SC is on. E-FLM is the exception — it keeps its strong 3e-4
  preference in both arms.
- **Geometry still matters, but the gap narrows**: with SC the ranking is
  S-FLM ≳ H-FLM > E-FLM (medium 92.6 / 91.0 / 82.7; hard 69.7 / 65.6 / 45.7)
  — sphere/hyperbolic hold a real edge over Euclidean even after SC.
- Eval note: hflm numbers use this branch's argmax-exact endpoint (see
  EXPERIMENT.md caveat); both SC arms and the reused curvature cells share
  that code path, so the deltas are internally consistent.
